Dry Eye Management

Overview

Dry eye is far more than just a minor nuisance; it is a recognized medical condition that can severely impact your quality of life, making it difficult to read, drive, or watch television comfortably. Your eyes rely on a constant, healthy layer of tears to stay lubricated and clear. Dry eye occurs when your eyes either do not produce enough tears, or the tears evaporate too quickly because they lack vital natural oils. Our goal is to find the exact root cause of your irritation and provide long-lasting relief.

Common Symptoms:

  • A "Gritty" Feeling: A persistent burning, stinging, or scratching sensation, as if there is sand in your eye.
  • Fluctuating Vision: Intermittent blurry vision that seems to clear up slightly when you blink rapidly.
  • Watery Eyes: Surprisingly, constantly watering eyes are often a primary symptom, as your eye creates emergency "reflex tears" to compensate for the underlying dryness.
